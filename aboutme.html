About Me

Personal Background

I was born in Boston, Massachusetts and raised in Los Angeles, California. I am a first-generation female immigrant from a Persian-American background. I am bilingual in Farsi and English—Farsi is my first language, but English is my primary language. Being both a product of diversity and an advocate for it, I’ve learned how to communicate across cultures, value different perspectives, and adapt in inclusive environments. These experiences shape how I connect with people, lead teams, and solve problems.

Education

I am currently pursuing a double Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Statistics at California State University, Northridge (CSUN), where I hold a 3.7 GPA. My journey began at El Camino Real Charter High School, where I discovered my passion for computer science after taking my first CS class in junior year. During high school, I also performed in the Percussion Ensemble and Marching Band, playing several instruments — with the marimba being my favorite. After graduating in 2020, I attended Los Angeles Pierce College for three years, building a strong foundation in computer science and math before transferring to CSUN. Encouraged by a statistics professor during my first semester, I decided to pursue a second major in statistics.

Hobbies

On my personal time, I focus on wellness. I enjoy hiking, walking, tennis, and going to the gym. I also enjoy traveling and playing billiards. I’ve been playing billiards competitively for over 3 years.
